Lesson 3: Speed Control and Obstacle Avoidance

Welcome to the third lesson of the Road Rally Course! In this lesson, we will focus on speed control and obstacle avoidance, which are crucial skills to master for any road rally driver.

Speed Control:

Maintaining a safe and consistent speed is critical for safety and efficiency during a rally. Here are some tips to help you control your speed:

  • Keep a steady foot on the accelerator and avoid sudden acceleration or deceleration.
  • Use engine braking instead of brakes when going downhill to maintain a consistent speed.
  • Keep an eye on the tachometer to ensure you're in the appropriate gear for the road conditions.
  • Avoid over-revving the engine or driving at speeds that exceed your comfort level.

Obstacle Avoidance:

Navigating around obstacles safely and efficiently is a vital skill for any rally driver. Here are some tips to help you avoid obstacles on the course:

  • Always be aware of your surroundings and look ahead for potential hazards.
  • Use the course map and notes to anticipate turns and obstacles.
  • Maintain a safe distance from other vehicles and objects on the course.
  • Practice defensive driving techniques such as swerving or braking to avoid obstacles.

Note:

Remember, safety should always be your top priority during a road rally. Mastering these skills will not only help you stay safe but also improve your overall performance on the course.
